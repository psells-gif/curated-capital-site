Understanding Asset-Backed Lending: A Collector's Guide

January 2026  ·  6 min read

How discerning collectors are leveraging their passion assets to access liquidity without parting with pieces they love.

← Back to Perspectives

For generations, collectors have faced a frustrating dilemma: when liquidity needs arise, they've often felt forced to choose between their financial requirements and the treasured pieces they've spent years—sometimes decades—carefully acquiring. Asset-backed lending offers a third path, one that's increasingly embraced by sophisticated collectors who understand that their passion and their financial flexibility need not be mutually exclusive.

What Is Asset-Backed Lending?

At its core, asset-backed lending is elegantly simple. Rather than selling a valuable asset to access its financial value, you use that asset as collateral for a loan. You receive capital based on a percentage of the asset's appraised value—typically 40-60% depending on the asset class and specific item—while maintaining ownership. Once the loan is repaid, your asset is returned to you.

This structure offers several distinct advantages over selling:

  • Retained ownership — Your piece remains yours. If it appreciates during the loan term, you benefit from that appreciation.
  • No capital gains realization — Unlike a sale, a loan doesn't trigger taxable events on appreciated assets.
  • Discretion — There's no public auction record, no market signal that you're divesting.
  • Speed — Loans can often be structured more quickly than orchestrating a private sale or consigning to auction.

Who Uses Asset-Backed Lending?

The clients we serve at Curated Capital come from diverse backgrounds, but they share certain characteristics. They're typically individuals who have built meaningful collections over time—not speculators, but genuine enthusiasts who understand and appreciate what they own. Their reasons for seeking liquidity are equally varied:

  • Business opportunities — Entrepreneurs who want to invest in their companies without liquidating personal assets
  • Real estate transactions — Collectors who need bridge financing for property purchases
  • Collection expansion — Leveraging existing pieces to acquire new ones without depleting cash reserves
  • Estate planning — Structured approaches to managing wealth across generations
  • Personal liquidity events — Weddings, education expenses, medical needs

What unites these scenarios is a preference for financial sophistication over the blunt instrument of selling. These collectors understand that parting with a cherished piece, particularly one that may be irreplaceable or have significant personal meaning, should be a last resort rather than a first response.

How the Process Works

While each lender structures their process differently, the general framework is consistent. Understanding these steps can help you approach the process with confidence.

1. Initial Consultation

The process begins with a conversation about your needs. What assets are you considering as collateral? What liquidity amount are you seeking? What's your preferred loan term? This discussion helps establish whether asset-backed lending is the right solution for your situation.

2. Valuation

Professional appraisal is fundamental to the process. For watches, this typically involves authentication, condition assessment, and market analysis. For art, it may include provenance research, condition reporting, and comparable sales analysis. For jewelry, gemological evaluation and market assessment. The goal is establishing a defensible current market value.

A Note on Loan-to-Value Ratios

Loan-to-value (LTV) ratios—the percentage of appraised value you can borrow—vary by asset class. Watches typically command 50-60% LTV due to their liquidity and standardized markets. Art may range from 40-50% given its more subjective valuation. Jewelry falls somewhere in between. These ratios reflect the lender's assessment of liquidation risk, not a judgment on your asset's quality.

3. Terms and Documentation

Once valuation is complete, you'll receive a loan proposal outlining the principal amount, interest rate, term, and any fees. Reputable lenders provide clear, straightforward documentation without hidden terms. Take time to review and ask questions—this is a significant financial arrangement.

4. Asset Transfer and Funding

Upon agreement, your asset is transferred to secure storage—typically a bonded, insured facility with appropriate climate control and security measures. Once received and verified, funds are disbursed, often within 24-48 hours.

5. Loan Term and Repayment

During the loan term, your asset remains safely stored. Interest may be paid monthly or accrued, depending on the loan structure. At term end, you repay the principal and any outstanding interest, and your asset is returned. Many loans offer early repayment options without penalty.

Choosing the Right Partner

Not all asset-backed lenders are created equal. When evaluating potential partners, consider:

  • Expertise — Does the lender have genuine knowledge of your asset class? Specialists will provide more accurate valuations and better understand your collection's nuances.
  • Transparency — Are terms clearly explained? Are there hidden fees? Reputable lenders welcome questions.
  • Storage and insurance — How and where will your asset be stored? What insurance coverage is provided? These details matter enormously.
  • Track record — How long has the lender been operating? What do other collectors say about their experience?
  • Discretion — Does the lender understand and respect the privacy considerations inherent in these transactions?

Common Questions

What happens if I can't repay the loan?
This is naturally the most common concern. Different lenders have different policies, but most work with borrowers experiencing difficulties to find solutions—loan extensions, restructured terms, or partial repayment arrangements. In the event of default, the lender has the right to sell the collateral to recover their principal, but this is genuinely a last resort. No reputable lender wants to liquidate your assets; they want to be repaid and to maintain a positive relationship.

Is my asset insured during the loan term?
Yes. Professional lenders maintain comprehensive insurance coverage for all assets in their custody. This should be clearly documented in your loan agreement, including coverage amounts and any exclusions.

Can I borrow against multiple items?
Absolutely. Many collectors prefer to distribute their collateral across several pieces rather than concentrating on a single high-value item. This approach can sometimes result in better overall terms.

How quickly can I get funding?
Timing varies based on the asset and logistics involved, but straightforward transactions can often be completed within a week. Watches and jewelry typically move fastest; art may require additional time for condition reporting and logistical arrangements.

Asset-backed lending isn't right for everyone or every situation. But for collectors who value both their financial flexibility and the integrity of their collections, it represents a sophisticated tool worth understanding. We're here whenever you'd like to explore what it might mean for you.
