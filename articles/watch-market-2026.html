The State of the Luxury Watch Market in 2026

January 2026  ·  8 min read

After years of unprecedented growth, the luxury watch market has entered a period of thoughtful recalibration. We examine what this means for collectors and those considering their timepieces as collateral.

← Back to Perspectives

The luxury watch market of 2026 presents a fascinating study in maturation. After the extraordinary price surges of 2021-2022, followed by the correction of 2023-2024, we now find ourselves in what many industry observers are calling a "new normal"—a market characterized by more rational pricing, discerning buyers, and a renewed focus on fundamental value.

Understanding the Current Landscape

To appreciate where we are, it's helpful to remember where we've been. During the peak of the watch market boom, certain references—particularly from Rolex, Patek Philippe, and Audemars Piguet—traded at multiples of their retail prices. Waiting lists stretched to years, even decades. The secondary market became, for many collectors, the only viable path to acquisition.

The correction that followed was inevitable, though its extent varied significantly by brand and reference. What's emerged is a more nuanced market where quality, provenance, and rarity command appropriate premiums, while speculation-driven pricing has largely dissipated.

"The collectors who weathered the correction are those who bought what they loved, not what they expected to flip."

What This Means for Collectors

For passionate collectors, the current environment offers several advantages. Pieces that were impossible to acquire just a few years ago are now more accessible. The frenzy has subsided, allowing for more thoughtful purchasing decisions. And importantly, the relationship between authorized dealers and collectors has begun to normalize.

However, this doesn't mean the market lacks opportunity. Certain categories continue to demonstrate strength:

  • Vintage pieces with documented provenance — Watches with compelling histories continue to appreciate, particularly those with original documentation and period-correct components.
  • Discontinued references — As production ends, desirability often increases, particularly for models that defined their era.
  • Independent watchmakers — Brands like F.P. Journe, MB&F, and De Bethune have maintained strong valuations, supported by limited production and devoted collector bases.
  • Condition-exceptional examples — In any market, truly pristine examples command premiums that transcend broader trends.

Watches as Collateral: A Rational Approach

For those considering their watch collection as a source of liquidity, the current market actually presents advantages. Valuations have stabilized, making it easier to understand and predict lending values. The volatility that characterized recent years has subsided, reducing the risk of significant value swings during a loan term.

At Curated Capital, we've observed that collectors increasingly view asset-backed lending not as a last resort, but as a sophisticated financial tool. Rather than selling a cherished piece, they can access its value while maintaining ownership—an approach that becomes particularly compelling when considering watches with strong emotional significance or anticipated long-term appreciation.

The most successful collectors understand that liquidity and passion need not be mutually exclusive. A well-structured loan allows you to leverage your collection's value without sacrificing the joy of ownership.

Looking Ahead

As we progress through 2026, several factors will shape the luxury watch market's trajectory. The major brands continue to manage supply carefully, maintaining an element of scarcity that supports valuations. Meanwhile, a new generation of collectors—many of whom entered during the recent boom—are developing more sophisticated approaches to building and managing their collections.

For collectors and investors alike, the key remains unchanged: focus on quality, buy what resonates with you personally, and take a long-term view. The watches that have proven most resilient through market cycles are those that combine exceptional craftsmanship with genuine desirability—qualities that transcend market sentiment.

Whether you're considering adding to your collection, evaluating your current holdings, or exploring how your timepieces might serve as collateral for other opportunities, we're here to provide perspective and guidance. The market may evolve, but the fundamental appreciation for exceptional watchmaking endures.
